# General Pet Care Guidelines

## Priority Framework for Scheduling
- **HIGH priority**: Medications, insulin injections, vet appointments, feeding (for pets with medical conditions)
- **MEDIUM priority**: Daily walks, play sessions, grooming, litter cleaning
- **LOW priority**: Baths, nail trims, enrichment activities, non-urgent cleaning

## Conflict Avoidance Tips
- Space feeding tasks at least 30 minutes apart if caring for multiple pets to avoid cross-contamination or stress.
- Never schedule two HIGH priority tasks at the same time.
- Grooming and walks should not overlap — pets need full attention for safety.
- Medication tasks should have a fixed, consistent time each day.

## Recurrence Recommendations
- **Daily recurrence**: Feeding, water changes, litter scooping, medication, walks
- **Weekly recurrence**: Brushing (short-hair), nail check, ear cleaning
- **Once (ONCE)**: Vet appointments, special treatments, one-off tasks

## Common Scheduling Mistakes to Avoid
1. Feeding too close to vigorous exercise (wait 30–60 min after eating before walks for dogs).
2. Forgetting to schedule medication refills — add a monthly reminder task.
3. Skipping dental care — it leads to expensive vet procedures.
4. Not scheduling enough enrichment for indoor pets — leads to behavioral issues.

## Task Duration Reference
| Task | Typical Duration |
|------|-----------------|
| Feeding | 10–20 min |
| Walk (short) | 20–30 min |
| Walk (long) | 45–60 min |
| Grooming/brushing | 15–30 min |
| Litter scoop | 5–10 min |
| Bath | 30–45 min |
| Nail trim | 10–15 min |
| Vet appointment | 60–90 min |
| Training session | 10–15 min |
| Medication | 5–10 min |
| Play session | 10–20 min |

## Emergency Signs — Schedule a Vet Visit Immediately
- Not eating for more than 24 hours
- Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 12 hours
- Lethargy combined with loss of appetite
- Difficulty breathing, limping, or eye discharge
- Unusual lumps or rapid weight changes
